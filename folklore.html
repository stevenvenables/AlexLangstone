Hello and welcome to Cornish Folklore!


Dydh da, lien Gwerinek a Gernow a'gas dynnergh!


Cornish Folklore - Lien Gwerinek a Gernow is a project which aims to record both known and newly discovered folklore from across the Duchy. Please get in touch if you would like to contribute.

email: cornish.folklore@yahoo.co.uk

Project coordinator: Alex Langstone

The ancient Celtic land of Cornwall has a wealth of recorded folklore. This blog has been set up to share knowledge of Cornish folklore, culture and related subjects. It is intended to run alongside the Cornish Folklore - Lien gwerinek a Gernow Facebook group and our twitter feed. Feel free to join us. Maybe you live nearby and know of some local lore that has been lost or maybe you are interested from afar or have experienced something otherworldly whilst visiting. Either way, if you are interested in the folklore, mysteries, culture, festivals and legends of Cornwall join our Facebook group, Twitter feed or come along to one of our events!

Research and Articles

I’m very interested in collecting experiences and ‘handed-down’ knowledge of Bodmin Moor and East Cornwall.
Have you (or someone you know) had a ghostly or supernatural experience on the moor? Did any of your elderly relatives have a saying or rhyme about an activity or place, maybe to do with china clay, tin or copper mining, fishing or a local farm? I’m interested in collecting any snippets, stories and anything else of a folkloric nature, no matter how insignificant it may seem to be. Please use the contact us link here contact us, if you have anything to pass on. Many thanks.
Alex.
